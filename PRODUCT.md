## Users

Guatemalans at home and abroad form the primary audience — people who recognize these words from family dinners, street markets, and daily life, and who feel a pang of cultural connection when they hear them. A secondary audience includes Spanish language learners, linguists studying Central American dialects, and tourists visiting Guatemala who want to understand local expressions. The site serves two surfaces: the Spanish experience for native speakers and the English experience for international visitors; both must feel equally complete and native, not like a translation of the other.

## Product Purpose

Chapinismos.org exists to preserve, celebrate, and make accessible the unique vocabulary of Guatemala — words, expressions, and idioms that carry centuries of cultural mestizaje. It is the definitive open-source reference for Guatemalan slang. Success means: a visitor finds the word they were looking for, learns something new about its cultural context, and leaves feeling more connected to Guatemala's linguistic heritage. The dictionary grows through community contribution, making every visitor a potential co-author.

## Positioning

The only bilingual, open-source, community-driven dictionary of Guatemalan slang — built with cultural pride, not academic sterility.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: Contribute a word or correction (grows the dictionary, deepens engagement)
- Secondary CTA: Browse the alphabetical index (explore the full catalog)
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "This is what it means to be chapín"
- Belief ladder:
  1. This is a real, well-maintained dictionary (structured data, clean design, working search)
  2. It understands my culture, not just my language (Guatemalan examples, cultural context, not generic translations)
  3. It's built by and for chapines (open source, contribution form, community-driven)
  4. I want to add a word my grandma taught me (contribution CTA)
- Proof on hand: Open-source GitHub repository with active contributions, 100+ word entries with Zod validation, bilingual ES/EN content, deployed on Vercel with strong SEO (sitemap, structured data, hreflang)

## Brand Personality

Voice: Warm but not sentimental, proud but not nationalistic, playful but not frivolous, authoritative but not academic. Think of a favorite tía who knows every word in the language and tells stories that make you laugh and cry in the same sentence. Three words: **cultured, warm, spirited**.

Emotional goals: Curious delight (finding unexpected words), community belonging (this is _our_ dictionary), confident authority (rigorously maintained), nostalgic warmth (hearing abuela's voice in a definition).

## Anti-references

- **Generic dictionaries** (Merriam-Webster, RAE): Sterile, clinical, academic. Chapinismos is alive, not encyclopedic.
- **SaaS dashboards**: Clean but soulless. No card grids, metric templates, or corporate palettes.
- **Favela-chic / poverty aesthetic**: Using "Guatemalan" as a license for rough, grungy, or patronizing design. Guatemala is vibrant, not a charity case.
- **Generic AI aesthetic**: Cream backgrounds, warm neutrals, gradient text headers, numbered section markers, identical card grids. This site must look like it was designed by someone who has actually been to Guatemala.

## Design Principles

1. **Culture-first decisions**: Every visual choice should answer "does this feel Guatemalan?" before "does this feel modern?" The flag colors, the warmth, the playfulness — these are design assets, not decorations.
2. **Language as living artifact**: Words are not static entries in a database. They are cultural bridges. The design should make each word feel like a small celebration, not a search result.
3. **Playful authority**: The content is rigorously validated (Zod, TypeScript, structured data). The presentation should be joyful. Rigor and delight are not opposites.
4. **Bilingual native**: Spanish and English are not "primary" and "secondary." Both languages get the same visual weight, the same care in typography, the same completeness of experience.
5. **Community co-authorship**: The contribution form is not an afterthought. It is a first-class feature that says "this dictionary belongs to everyone."

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA target. The project already implements skip-to-content links, keyboard navigation, ARIA labels, reduced motion support, and semantic HTML. Continue building on this foundation: ensure all interactive elements are keyboard-accessible, color contrast meets AA thresholds (including the blue primary on dark backgrounds), and the bilingual experience is screen-reader friendly with proper `lang` attributes and hreflang tags.
